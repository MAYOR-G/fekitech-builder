# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Small-business owners, independent professionals, and agencies who need to create, customize, save, and publish websites without managing hosting infrastructure or writing code. Their primary workflow is selecting a template, adapting its content and visual settings, previewing the result, and publishing it to an isolated tenant hostname.

## Product Purpose

FekiTech Builder provides a dependable multi-user website-building workflow with server-enforced ownership, plan entitlements, reusable templates, safe asset management, draft history, and controlled publishing. Success means users can trust that saved work is durable, paid capabilities are enforced consistently, and no account can access another account's private projects or assets.

## Brand Personality

Approachable, capable, and trustworthy. Product copy should be direct and reassuring, with clear state and error feedback rather than decorative or exaggerated claims.

## Anti-references

Avoid interfaces that advertise unavailable controls, conceal failures behind optimistic status text, use decoration in place of hierarchy, or make standard editing and account actions unfamiliar. Do not redesign the established identity during the reliability phase.

## Design Principles

1. Trust is a feature: saving, publishing, plan status, and errors must always reflect server truth.
2. Server enforcement comes first: the interface explains permissions but never substitutes for authorization.
3. Familiar workflows beat novelty: editing, previews, versions, uploads, and account controls use recognizable product patterns.
4. Progressive capability: unavailable features are disabled or removed until their complete workflow exists.
5. Tenant boundaries remain explicit from database query to rendered asset.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for product surfaces. All functionality must be keyboard accessible, expose programmatic names and labels, meet text contrast requirements, provide reduced-motion behavior, and remain usable on touch screens and narrow viewports.
